2025-10-17 博士英语口语课第四节

这节课主要讲语音学(phonetics)–声音怎么发出来和音韵学(phonology)–不同声音的规则变化中的音韵学部分.

课程资料

Note

主要内容

tone language vs. intonation language

世界上的语言大致可以分为两类: tone language声调语言以及intonation language语调语言.

  • 声调语言: 音节内部的音高变化会用来区分词义. 例如: 中文的阴平, 阳平, 上声, 去声;
  • 语调语言: 音节内部音高变化不用来区分词意义, 用整个句子的语调来表达说话人的情绪, 语气等. 例如: 英语.
    我们的日常生活中使用中文时, 很少使用整句话的语调变化和肢体语言及表情表达词意, 我们更多使用的是声调(阴阳上去)就可以区分词意; 而英文中, 不光大量使用肢体语言及表情表达词意, 还会使用整句话的语调变化来表达说话人的情绪和语气. 但是在英语中, 单个音节是几乎没有语调变化的, 可以认为统一使用一个声调–对应中文近似认为是上声(三声).

注: 标准中文具有4+1(轻声)个声调, 粤语有12个声调.

节拍

英语并不是每个单词都需要按照三声去读, 而是有重读和轻读之分. 作为整句话, 就要读的有节奏. 例如以下四句话:

  • Dogs eat bones.
  • The dogs eat the bones.
  • The dogs will eat the bones.
  • The dogs will have eaten the bones.

按照第一句话, 应该有三个节拍, 而这四句话节拍是相同的. 所以这四句话实际上按照相同的节奏去读. 为了保证节拍, 一些单词要进行轻读和连读. 例如这里的”the”需要轻读, 而这里的”will have” 连读成/ləv/, 即”dogs will have”读成””dogs ləv”来保证节拍.

重读和轻读

英语句子中的单词有重读和轻读之分, 刨除一些例外情况, 一般情况总结如下:

需要重读的词性(part of speech): nouns, (main) verbs, adjectives, adverbs, demonstrative words(this, that, these, those), wh-words(what, who, where, when, why, how)

需要轻读的词性: prepositions, conjunctions, articles(冠词), interjections(感叹词), pronouns, auxiliary verbs(助动词), modal verbs(情态动词)

注: 情态动词不能轻易重读, 重读表示输出情绪.

训练:

  • I can do it.
  • I can’t do it.

由于英语读起来不是很硬, 不会读出来/t/的爆破音, 所以经常就会把”can’t”读成”can”. 导致两种情况很难区分. 但是如果重音放到”do”上, 就代表第一种; 重音放到”can’t”上, 就代表第二种.

注: 中文因为很少有略读, 一般只有重读和重重读; 英语有略读, 所以一些本来不用重读的音, 如果没有略读(重读或者延长时值), 即视为强调.

违反以上规则的一些特殊情况:

  1. 第一次提到一般强调名词, 第二次提到一般强调动词.

例如:
Dogs eat bones.
They eat bones and keep bones as well.

  1. 对比

例如:
Bob studies English, but he doesn’t use it.

  1. 强调

例如:

  • I didn’t say he stole the money.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money.

指的是:

  • I didn’t say he stole the money, someone else did.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money, that’s not true at all.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money, I only suggested the possibility.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money, I think someone else took it.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money, maybe he just borrowed it.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money, but rather some other money.
  • I didn’t say he stole the money, he may have taken some jewellery.
  1. 复合名词

如果是一个形容词修饰的词组, 一般重读放到后面的名词上, 例如:

  • a nice guy
  • a big house
  • a good idea
  • a white house

如果是一个复合名词, 一般重读放到前面的名词上, 例如:

  • a greenhouse(温室)
  • a hotdog
  • the White House(白宫)
  1. 非重读出词放最后一般重读.

例子:
I will go on and on.

一些特殊例子:

重音放第一个单词
set phrases light bulb
streets Main Street
Co. or Corp. Xerox Corporation
nationalities of food Chinese food
nationalities of people French guy

descriptive phrases new information
road designations Fifth Avenue
modified adjectives really big
place names and parks New York, Central Park
institutions, or Inc. Oakland Museum, Xerox Inc.
personal names and titles Bob Smith, Assistant Manager
personal pronouns and possessives his car, Bob’s brother

colors and numbers red orange, 26
most compound verbs go away, sit down, fall off

注: 语言学这种并非先验性规则, 而是描述性规则. 很多规则并非绝对, 而是约定俗成, 约定来源于生活.

例子:

  • a nice guy
  • an American guy
  • an American restaurant
  • American food
  • an American teacher
  • an English teacher

测验: 标清重音以及断句

Hello, my name is _________. I’m taking American Accent Training. There’s a lot to learn, but I hope to make it as enjoyable as possible. I should pick up on the American intonation pattern pretty easily, although the only way to get it is to practice all of the time. I use the up and down, or peaks and valleys intonation more than I used to. I’ve been paying attention to pitch, too. It’s like walking down a staircase. I’ve been talking to a lot of Americans lately, and they tell me that I’m easier to understand. Anyway, I could go on and on, but the important thing is to listen well and sound good. Well, what do you think? Do I?

音频听力如下:

答:
Hello,/ my name is _________./ I’m taking American Accent Training./ There’s a lot to learn,/ but I hope to make it/ as enjoyable as possible./ I should pick up on/ the American intonation pattern/ pretty easily,/ although/ the only way to get it is/ to practice all of the time./ I use the up and down,/ or peaks and valleys intonation/ more than I used to./ I’ve been paying attention to pitch,/ too./ It’s like walking down a staircase./ I’ve been talking/ to a lot of Americans/ lately,/ and they tell me/ that I’m easier/ to understand./ Anyway,/ I could go on and on,/ but the important thing is/ to listen well/ and sound good./ Well,/ what do you think?/ Do I?

注: 断句一般在to之前停顿; 在系动词后面停顿.
